#### Definition

Courts have power to hear and dispute. Disputes are either criminal or civil in
nature.

### Structure of the Kenyan Court System

| Rank     | Court
|----------|---------------|
| Superior | Supreme Court |
|          | Court of Appeal |
|          | High Court |
| Subordinate | Magistrate Court |
|             | Kadhis Court |
|             | Court Martial | 
|             | Any other tribunal court |

#### Supreme Court 

It is the highest court in the judiciary. The court hears and determines cases
related to:

1. Presidential petition under article 140.
2. Appeals from the court of appeal.
3. Issue advisory opinions on matters concerning
4. Cases involving interpretation or application of the consititution.

Comprises of the chief justice who is the also the president of the court.
Deputy chief justice -> vice president
5 judges

#### Court of Appeal

Handles appeals from high court artcle 164(2). Comprises of a max of 30 judges.
They elect the prisdent amongest themselves. Has been decentralized.
1. Nairobi
2. Mombasa
3. Nyeri
4. Kisumu
5. Nakuru
6. Eldorect

#### High Court

Jurisdiction to hear all criminal and civil cases as well as appeals from the 
lower courts.
Comprises of a max of 150 judges.
Premire court in intrepreting the consititution.
Supervises all the administrative police i.e judicial review

##### High Court Divisions

1. Familiy Division
2. Commercial and Admiralty
3. Consititutional and Judicial Review
4. Land and Environment Revision
5. Criminal Division

There are atleast 20 high court stations country wide.

#### Magistrates Court

It deals with the majority of cases in Kenya.
116 court stattions maned 155 magistrates. Authority to hear all criminal cases
except
1. Murder
2. Treson
3. Crime under Criminal Law

#### Kadhis Court

Their jurisdiction is limited to determination of questions of muslim law leading
to:
1. Personal Status
2. Marriage
3. Divorce
4. Inheritance

#### Court Martial

It hears cases people serving in the military (Read Armed Forces Act).

#### Tribunal

It is established by the acts of Parliament to exercise judicial or quasi-judicial
function.

It supplements ordinal courts in the administration of justice.

Don't have penal jurisdiction (can't send to prision).

1. Political parties dispute tribunal.
2. Sports dispute.
3. HIV/AIDS
4. Rent restriction tribunal.
5. Energy Tribunal
6. Education Appeal


### Court Room Participants

#### Police

They ensure an orderly court room and provides security. They also serve as witnesses.

#### Court Clerk

Maintains all records of criminal cases. Also supports the work of the court in
other ways such as scheduling hearings or assisting with the paper work.

#### Magistrate / Judge

A public offcial who holds ulitimate authority, ensures justice and safe guard
the rights of the accused, rights of the victims and the public.

#### Public Reporter

They are responsible for recording and transcribing court proceedings. They create
an offcial record of the trial.

#### Expert Witness

They are individuals with specialized knowledge or skills relevant to the case.
They provide professional opinions and analysis to assit the court in understanding
complex issues.

#### Lay Witness (Non-expert)

They provide factual information about events or circumstances relevant to the 
case. They testify based on their personal experiences or observations.

#### Victim

It is the person who has allegedly suffered harm due to a crime. They may provide
testimony about the incident and its impact on them.

#### Prosecutors

They represent the government and are responsible for presenting the case against
the defendant. They aim to prove the defendants guilt.

#### Defense Council (Lawyers)

They represent the defendant and works to challenge the prosecutions and arguments
in favour of the defendant.

#### Defendant

It is the person accused of a crime. They respond to the charges, either admitting 
or denying guilt.

If they deny guilt, they have the right to a defense.

#### Spectators and the press

Spectators are observers in the court room, while the press includes journalists
and reporters covering the proceedings.

They ensure transparency and public awareness in the legal process.

#### Probation/Parole Officers

They may provide pre-sentencing reports, monitor defendants who receive probation
and offer recommendation for sentencing.

#### Children Officers

Focus on cases involving minors, ensuring their welfare and representing their 
interests in court, particularly in matters of child protection and custody.
